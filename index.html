The experience

How Third Works.

We’ve removed every barrier between you and a new connection.

1
Find events near you

Explore a monthly calendar of interest-based events in your neighborhood. Join in on book swaps, craft nights, game nights, and more!

2
RSVP

Use your monthly subscription credits or pay per event. Events are capped at 30 people — if the one you wanted is full, no worries! Events recur at the same local venues every week, so just grab a spot at the next one.

3
Let the activity do the work

A facilitator guides the event with icebreakers and structure built right in. Just show up, enjoy the activity, and meet the people around you. Come back next week and find familiar faces waiting.

What makes us different

Not another event app.
A community infrastructure.

Same place, every time

We partner with neighborhood venues — a bookshop, a café, a game cafe — and anchor events there weekly. Familiarity builds comfort. Comfort builds community.

Actually affordable

Events are $25 or less, with at least one free event every month. If you’re coming back regularly, a $35 monthly subscription gets you four events.

Intimacy at scale

A hard cap of 30 attendees means no lost-in-the-crowd moments. Events are designed around smaller pods within the group — so even if you show up alone, you’ll have a place to sit.

Who we are

“The goal isn’t more events. It’s the moment a stranger becomes a familiar face.”

Third was born out of a simple observation: the places where community used to happen organically still exist, but the behavior doesn’t. People sit together without speaking. We shrink ourselves in shared spaces. We’re building the infrastructure that brings the conversation back.

At Third, we believe human connection, a sense of belonging, and real community aren’t nice-to-haves — they’re the foundation of a life well lived. And in a city of eight million people, no one should feel like they’re living it alone. We’re building a place for exactly that.

Meghan Gaur
Founder & CEO

After the pandemic, I found myself back in my hometown realizing that the community I’d built over the years had quietly disappeared. Without the structure of school or in-person work, I didn’t know where to look for community, for friends. So I started hosting bring-a-friend events at a local park in San Diego, and almost everyone I met there told me they felt lost navigating friendships in adulthood too.

I believe community has always been around us — in our neighborhoods, in the spaces we share every day. We’ve just lost the ability to foster it. Through Third, I hope to bring that back.

Columbia Business School MBA candidate, Entrepreneurship and Marketing.
